COVID-19 Spread the World

COVID-19 is a new disease, caused by a novel (or new) coronavirus that has not previously been seen in humans. Because it is a new virus, scientists are learning more each day. Although most people who have COVID-19 have mild symptoms, COVID-19 can also cause severe illness and even death.

After the first case of COVID-19 been identified in December 2019, the World Health Organization (WHO) sooner declared it a pandemic on March 11th as the number of affected countries continues to rise. Over a year of the COVID-19 pandemic outbreak, up till March 10, 2021, United States has reached a total case of 29,798,657, which made up about 9% of the total population in the United States.

Consumer Price and Producer Price during COVID-19(p8)

Fluctuation of Consumer Price and Producer Price

The graph describes the immediate impact of COVID-19 Cases on two of the economic indicators: Consumer Price Index and Producer Price. By selecting a time period on Producer scatter plot, we can visualize the corresponding consumer prices during the same time interval. In the graph, note that there is a drastic decrease of the index, represented by the bar has a negative value highlighted in red color. This happened just after when the COVID-19 became a serious case in the United States, so we can conclude that the pandemic indeed has a negative impact on these two economic indicators.
